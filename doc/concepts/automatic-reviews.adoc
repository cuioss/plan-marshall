= Automatic Reviews
:nofooter:
:toc: left
:toclevels: 2

xref:../../README.md[Plan Marshall] » xref:README.adoc[Concepts]

Stock review processes ask the LLM to remember every comment, every Sonar issue, every CI failure on its way to merge. That memory is unreliable. Plan Marshall does not lean on it: every quality signal — a Q-Gate finding on the outline, a build error, a CI check that turned red, a Sonar issue, a PR review comment — becomes a row in one append-only store, runs through one triage envelope, and produces one of four documented resolutions. The next phase boundary actually reads the store before it advances.

== One mechanism, many producers

image::../resources/diagrams/findings-pipeline.svg[Findings pipeline — producer scripts on the left (Q-Gate validation, build/verify, pre-submission self-review, CI verify, automated review, Sonar roundtrip, /workflow-pr-doctor) write into the manage-findings store in the middle; consumers on the right (qgate-resolve for the analytical reviews, verification-feedback envelope plus per-domain ext-triage-{domain} for everything else) query and resolve. The blocking-count invariant gates phase transitions., align=center]

Seven review surfaces feed the pipeline — Q-Gate over outline and task plan, build / verify during execute, structural self-review just before the PR, CI verify, automated PR-comment review, Sonar roundtrip, and the on-demand `/workflow-pr-doctor` sweep. All seven write into the same JSONL store. All seven flow through the same per-finding triage envelope, which ends with one of four resolutions: `fixed`, `suppressed`, `accepted`, or `taken_into_account`.

The producer side is deliberately mechanical — build logs parsed by regex, Sonar issues fetched via REST, PR comments fetched through the provider abstraction, the Q-Gate validators producing their candidate surface. LLM judgement enters only at the triage step, after the finding is in the store. That separation is what makes review reproducible: a reviewer comparing two runs sees the *same* signals reach the same triage loop, in the same shape.

Per-domain decision knowledge plugs in through the `ext-triage-{domain}` extension point — each domain bundle ships its own table mapping severity to outcome and its own suppression syntax. The triage workflow loads the right domain's standards at dispatch time, keyed by the finding's `file_path`. The orchestrator does not pick the rules; the domain that owns the file does.

[CAUTION]
====
**The four outcomes mean "addressed," not "the underlying issue went away."**

Only `fixed` removes the problem. `suppressed` puts an inline annotation in the source and explains why the warning is being told to stop. `accepted` documents a decision in the finding record without touching the code. `taken_into_account` records that the feedback shaped a higher-order change — typically an outline restructure or a scope adjustment. The boundary gate counts `pending` resolutions and refuses to advance while any remain; it does not relitigate whether the resolutions were the right calls. That stays the reviewer's job.
====

== On-demand review: recipe-code-review

The seven producers above are *automatic* — they fire at a phase boundary, on a build, or on a CI/PR event without anyone asking. The link:../../marketplace/bundles/plan-marshall/skills/recipe-code-review/SKILL.md[`recipe-code-review`] recipe is the *on-demand* counterpart: a diff-aware structural/quality review the operator invokes (`/plan-marshall recipe=code-review`) over the current footprint, bounded to an optional path/file scope and an optional explicit base branch.

It is an eighth producer, not a replacement. The recipe emits its observations as `lint-issue` findings into the *same* `manage-findings` store and dispatches each to its domain `ext-triage-{domain}` extension — so an on-demand review flows through the identical triage envelope, four-outcome resolution model, and blocking-count gate as every automatic producer. It complements the automated PR-comment review and the Sonar roundtrip rather than standing apart from them: the operator can ask for a focused structural pass at any point, and whatever it surfaces lands in the one store the next phase boundary reads.

The security sibling, link:../../marketplace/bundles/plan-marshall/skills/recipe-security-audit/SKILL.md[`recipe-security-audit`], is the same shape for the security lens — an on-demand producer emitting `bug` / `anti-pattern` findings into the same pipeline (see xref:security.adoc[Concepts › Security]). Its *automatic* counterpart, `default:finalize-step-security-audit`, runs the same engine at finalize on every plan that touched files, taking its place in the phase-6 step sequence at `order: 9` — after `finalize-step-simplify`, before `push`, ahead of the automated PR-comment review and the Sonar roundtrip. Like the recipe it emits `bug` / `anti-pattern` findings into the one store, so the proactive gate flows through the identical triage envelope and four-outcome resolution as every other producer.
